    🌍 SDG 2030 relevance: Africa's data revolution

    The African Union’s Agenda 2063 and the SDGs require granular, timely data. Mobile devices directly address the "data gap" across the continent:

    • SDG 17.18 – Capacity building: 25+ African NSOs now use mobile platforms (Senegal, Ghana, Zambia).
    • Leaving no one behind – Pastoralists, urban poor, and women reached via offline mobile surveys.
    • Geospatial mapping – Every response anchored to GPS, enabling inequality tracking at ward level.
    • Conflict & fragile contexts – Mobile data works where traditional infrastructure is weak (Sahel, Great Lakes).
    🇨🇮 Côte d'Ivoire: Mobile-based household survey increased SDG indicator coverage by 41% in remote northern regions.
    📈 Compiling SDG indicators using Africa's mobile ecosystem

    National Bureaus of Statistics (NBS) are leveraging mobile tech for:

    1. Rapid enumeration – CAPI reduces errors and accelerates SDG reporting cycles.
    2. IVR & SMS polling – Reaching populations with low literacy (Nigeria, Malawi).
    3. Geospatial + mobile fusion – Mapping SDG 11 (urban slums) via citizen reporting.
    4. Gender-disaggregated data – Women-only mobile interviews increase response rates by 33%.
    5. Integration with Africa Data Hub – seamless upload to continental platforms.

    ✅ 65% of Tier I SDG indicators in Africa can now be informed by mobile-assisted methods.

    📲 African innovations: Beyond surveys
    • 📡 Passive mobile data – telecom metadata for mobility patterns (SDG 11 – urban planning in Senegal).
    • 📸 AI on feature phones – crop disease detection via WhatsApp (Ghana, Kenya).

    ⚡ SPEED

    From weeks to hours: mobile surveys in Rwanda enabled same-day reporting on COVID-19 impacts, guiding cash transfers within days.

    💰 COST-EFFICIENCY

    World Bank study: mobile CAPI reduces survey costs by 55-70% in sub-Saharan Africa compared to paper-based methods.

    🧑‍🤝‍🧑 INCLUSIVITY

    Mobile reaches women (mobile phone ownership: 84% in Africa), youth, and persons with disabilities through accessible interfaces.
